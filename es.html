Human Circulatory and Respiratory Systems

The circulatory and respiratory systems are vital for life.
The circulatory system transports blood, nutrients, oxygen, and hormones throughout the body, while the respiratory system allows us to breathe, providing oxygen and removing carbon dioxide.

Circulatory Parts and Functions

Heart: The heart is a muscular organ that pumps blood throughout the body.

Arteries: Blood vessels that carry oxygenated blood away from the heart to the body's tissues.

Veins: Blood vessels that return deoxygenated blood from the body's tissues back to the heart.

Capillaries: Tiny blood vessels that connect arteries and veins, facilitating exchange of gases and nutrients.

Blood: The fluid that carries oxygen, nutrients, hormones, and waste products.

Main Pulses of the Circulatory System

  • Carotid artery: Located in the neck, on either side of the windpipe.
  • Radial artery: Located on the thumb side of the wrist.
  • Brachial artery: Located in the upper arm, used to measure blood pressure.
  • Femoral artery: Located in the groin.
  • Popliteal artery: Located behind the knee.
  • Dorsalis pedis artery: Located on the top of the foot.

Respiratory System

Upper Respiratory Tract

Nose and nasal cavity: Warms, moisturizes, and filters air.

Mouth and oral cavity: A secondary external opening for air.

Sinuses: Hollow spaces that regulate air temperature and humidity.

Pharynx (throat): A muscular funnel for air passage.

Larynx (voice box): Produces sound and allows speech.

Lower Respiratory Tract

Trachea: Windpipe that carries air to the lungs.

Bronchi: Two tubes leading into each lung.

Bronchioles: Smaller branches of the bronchi.

Lungs: Organs that exchange oxygen and carbon dioxide.

Alveoli: Tiny sacs where gas exchange occurs.
